# Case Study: Cyberbullying

## Overview
Cyberbullying is the act of using the internet, social media, or messaging apps to harass, intimidate, or humiliate another person. This can include sending harmful messages, spreading false rumors, or sharing embarrassing images without permission. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can happen 24/7 and can reach a wider audience very quickly.

## How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The bully identifies a target, often a peer, classmate, or colleague.
2. Hurtful messages, offensive posts, or private images are shared online.
3. Friends or followers may amplify the content by sharing or commenting.
4. The victim repeatedly sees negative messages, which increases emotional stress.
5. The situation may escalate if the bully continues or if bystanders participate.

## Who Is Usually Targeted
Teenagers and young adults are most frequently targeted. Individuals who are very active on social media, share personal information, or are more visible online are at higher risk of cyberbullying.

## Consequences
Victims may experience anxiety, depression, social isolation, or a drop in academic/work performance. In severe cases, cyberbullying may lead to self-harm or legal consequences for the bully. It can also damage reputations and relationships.

## Prevention Measures
- Adjust privacy settings and limit sharing personal information online.
- Block or report abusive users on social media platforms.
- Promote awareness about the impact of cyberbullying among peers.
- Encourage open communication with family, friends, or counselors if harassment occurs.
